Featured Author: Fyodor Dostoyevsky

Fyodor Mikhailovich Dostoyevsky

Crime and Punishment

The Brothers Karamazov

Fyodor Mikhailovich Dostoyevsky(1821-1881) born in St. Petersburg was one of the most prolific writers of the Russian Golden Age of Literature. He led a traumatic life, his mother died at an early age, he was condemned to death, he was put to hard labor in Siberia, one might say that his painful life led to beautiful words on the paper. He's most well-known for his work "Crime and Punishment" a novel that deals with alienation, free will, crime, morality, and the human condition in general. It follows the story of a young lawyer, Rodion Raskolnikov, and his mental anguish after murdering his landlord. He is also known for other works like "The Brothers Karamazov", "The Idiot", "Demons", and his many novellas. His works expose the vulnerable underbelly of Russia in the 19th century, he showcases corruption, alcoholism, nihilism, and general moral decay. His philosophy revolves around the Russian Orthodox Church, his protagonists have desirable Orthodox traits. They are God-fearing, hopeful, perfectly moral, and a force combatting evil. Notable examples include Alyosha from The Brothers Karamazov and Prince Myshkin from The Idiot. He is joined by Leo Tolstoy as one of the most prolific Russian authors of the Russian literary canon.
